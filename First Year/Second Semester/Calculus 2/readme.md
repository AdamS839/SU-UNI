# Calculus 2

## Overview

The professor is very friendly and approachable, and is always willing to answer questions related to the course material. The subject itself is significantly more difficult than C1, although many of the definitions and theorems are quite similar. At the moment (2024/2025), some topics still do not have presentation slides prepared by the professor, so the material for those sections comes from a textbook written by another professor. Since there is a lot of content to cover, I would recommend attending the lectures and taking your own notes. This allows you to hear the lecturer’s explanations directly while also having the textbook as a second reference. Personally, I found the lecture notes easier to follow and understand than the textbook. Also, try to earn as many bonus points as possible for the theory exam, since they can be very useful later on.

## Subject Overview

Build upon the integration concepts introduced in the first semester and continue with definite integrals, including Riemann and Darboux integrals and their properties. After that, the course moves on to power series, followed by functions of several variables, where you study differentiation and integrability in higher dimensions. Many of the theorems covered are extensions of concepts already introduced in the first semester for single-variable functions, such as the theorems of Karl Weierstrass, Pierre de Fermat, Brook Taylor, and Joseph-Louis Lagrange.
